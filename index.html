THE PROBLEM

India's EV Battery Crisis

Millions of EV batteries will retire by 2030 — still holding 70–80% capacity with nowhere to go.

🔋
Premature Retirement
EV batteries are considered "end-of-life" at 80% SOH, yet hold years of usable storage potential for stationary applications.
☠️
Toxic Waste Risk
Improper disposal of lithium-ion batteries leaches heavy metals into soil and groundwater across India.
💰
Missed Value
₹2,000+ crore worth of usable battery capacity is lost annually to poor end-of-life management practices.
🏫
Campus Energy Gap
India's 50,000+ campuses rely on diesel generators for backup. Second-life batteries can eliminate this at 40% lower cost.

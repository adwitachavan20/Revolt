THE PROBLEM

India's EV Battery Crisis

Millions of EV batteries will retire by 2030 — still holding 70–80% capacity with nowhere to go.

🔋
Premature Retirement
EV batteries are considered "end-of-life" at 80% SOH, yet still have years of useful storage potential remaining.
☠️
Toxic Waste Risk
Improper disposal of lithium-ion batteries leaches heavy metals into soil and groundwater across India.
💰
Missed Value
₹2,000+ crore worth of usable battery capacity is lost annually to poor end-of-life management.
⚡
Storage Gap
India's solar revolution is stalled by expensive storage. Second-life batteries can bridge this gap at 40% lower cost.
THE SOLUTION
